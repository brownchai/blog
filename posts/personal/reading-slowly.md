---
title: "Reading Slowly"
date: "2024-12-14"
description: "A case for finishing fewer books and understanding more of them."
---

I used to keep a reading log. Titles, dates finished, a brief note. The implicit goal was to read more books per year — to have a larger number at the end of December.

I've abandoned the log. Here's why.

## The metric was wrong

When your goal is books-per-year, you optimise for finishing. You skim the slow parts. You push through sections that confuse you rather than stopping to figure out why. You choose shorter books or lighter ones.

The number goes up. The understanding goes sideways.

I notice this in conversations. I could tell you that I'd read a book without being able to tell you what it actually argued, or why it mattered, or what I thought was wrong with it. I'd consumed it without digesting it.

## What reading slowly looks like

Reading slowly means stopping when something doesn't make sense and working out why before continuing. It means rereading paragraphs. It means putting the book down to think.

It means, sometimes, not finishing books. If I'm a third of the way through something and it's not earning my continued attention, I stop. This used to feel like failure. Now it feels like discernment.

> The goal is understanding, not completion.

## The books that stay

There are books I read in a rush that I can barely recall. There are books I read slowly, with a pencil, returning to certain pages repeatedly, that I still think about years later.

The slow ones stay. The ones where I argued with the author in the margins, where I wrote down questions I didn't know how to answer — those are the ones that actually changed how I think.

## A practical note

I've started reading one book at a time, properly, rather than the four-books-at-once approach I used to favour. Less stimulating in a surface way. More useful in the ways that matter.

I've also started rereading. The idea that a book can only be read once is a strange one — why would a second reading not teach you different things than the first, now that you know where it's going?

The number on my reading log is smaller this year. I think I've read more.

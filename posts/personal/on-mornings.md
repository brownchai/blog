There's a particular quality to the hour before anyone else is awake. The light is thin and honest. The coffee is the first thing. The silence has texture.

I've been waking up earlier this year — not as a productivity intervention, not to get more done, but because I noticed I was happier on the days it happened by accident. This took me longer to act on than it should have.

## What mornings are actually for

The usual case for early waking is output: you'll write more, exercise, meditate, plan your day. I've found all of this to be true in a shallow way and beside the point in a deeper way.

The thing mornings give me isn't time for tasks. It's time before I become reactive. Every notification, message, and request pulls me into someone else's frame. The early morning is the one block of the day that belongs to me before it belongs to anyone else.

I don't do anything especially noble with it. I make coffee slowly. I read things that aren't about my job. Sometimes I write. Often I just sit and think about nothing in particular.

> The silence has texture.

That texture is rare. Protecting it feels like an act of self-preservation.

## The discipline of it

I won't pretend it's easy to maintain. There are weeks when I slide back to old hours, usually when I've stayed up too late reading or watching something. The mornings don't happen by force of will; they happen by protecting the evenings that precede them.

This is, I think, a general principle. The good habits we actually keep are usually downstream of other choices made hours earlier.

## What I've noticed

On the mornings I get this right, the rest of the day feels different. Not more productive necessarily — just more mine. I arrive at my desk with something already thought through, some corner of the world already explored. The day's demands feel smaller.

On the mornings I get it wrong, I start the day in reaction mode, which tends to be where I stay.

I have no grand theory of mornings. I just think the quiet is worth protecting, and that it's easy to let it go without noticing.
